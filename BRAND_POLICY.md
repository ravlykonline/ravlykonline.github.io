# Brand Policy

This repository makes a distinction between source code, educational content, and project branding.

## Scope

This policy applies to brand elements associated with the RAVLYK project, including:

- the names `RAVLYK` and `РАВЛИК`
- the domain `ravlyk.org`
- logos
- the snail character identity
- official visual identity and product presentation

## What this policy means

The source code and educational content may be reused under their respective licenses, but the project brand is not granted as part of those licenses.

Without separate written permission from the project author, you may not:

- present a fork or derivative product as the official RAVLYK project
- publish a modified version under the names `RAVLYK` or `РАВЛИК`
- use the official logo, character identity, or branding in a way that implies endorsement or official status
- register confusingly similar domains, package names, or public project identities that suggest your version is the original project

## What is allowed

You may:

- say that your project is based on RAVLYK
- link to the original repository or official site
- use factual references such as `forked from RAVLYK` or `based on RAVLYK`

## Derivative origin disclosure

Any public product, service, or distribution that presents itself
under its own name but is substantially based on the RAVLYK source
code or educational content must make clear in its public description
or documentation that it is based on RAVLYK, with a link to ravlyk.org.

This applies to branding and public positioning, not to every internal
or incidental use of the code or content. A teacher building a paid
course that uses RAVLYK materials satisfies the attribution requirement
of CC BY-SA 4.0 by crediting the source in the course materials; no
additional end-user banner or link is required beyond what CC BY-SA 4.0
itself specifies.

The requirement targets products and services that could mislead users
into thinking they are using an independent tool with no connection to
RAVLYK.

Acceptable forms of disclosure in product descriptions:

- "Based on RAVLYK (ravlyk.org)"
- "Built on RAVLYK by Artem Kysliakov"
- "Fork of RAVLYK — ravlyk.org"

## Recommended naming for forks

If you publish a modified public version, use a distinct name and clearly identify it as an independent fork.

Good examples:

- `ProjectName, based on RAVLYK`
- `ProjectName (fork of RAVLYK)`

Avoid names that make users think your version is the official project.

## No trademark registration claim

This policy does not claim that `RAVLYK` or `РАВЛИК` is a registered trademark.
It states the project's intended brand-usage rules for this repository and related public distributions.
